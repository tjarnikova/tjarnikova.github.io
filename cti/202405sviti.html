May has been a month of small domestic joys, flowers, and sun, which after a long cold lonely spring was more than welcome. We all looked at the northern lights and took grainy pictures of them on our smartphone and put them on our little instagram accounts, and maybe I would normally find that kitschy but I thought it was great.
• I finished a draft of a paper based on {this} talk. I had a negroni and an egg to celebrate, because, why not.

• I spent a few days waffling about asking a question about a dataset because I assumed I was missing something first-order / it was trivial to ‘the community’. When I finally did, it turns out the answer was, whilst understandable, not obvious, and that moreover the number of people in the world who could answer it is definitely less than 10. It was a reminder to ask questions, eff the culture that teaches us, from middle school, to feel dumb about it. (It’s changing. Good.)
• A different reminder in the form of the ghoul (self-satisfied retired engineer) who sat next to me on a plane: Don’t tell people who they are or what their problem is, unless they’ve explicitly asked you (this goes even if you know them well — perhaps doubly so).
