Tereza Jarníková
Hi! I'm a physical oceanographer using numerical models to study the ocean's changing carbon cycle. Currently I am a Senior Research Associate in the Green Ocean Modelling group, headed by Dr. Corinne Le Quéré at the University of East Anglia. My current work focuses on understanding the role of the changing ozone layer in the evolution of the Southern Ocean carbon sink. I recently finished a doctorate with Dr. Susan E. Allen and Dr. Debby Ianson at the University of British Columbia. During my doctorate, I developed a submesoscale carbonate chemistry model for the Salish Sea and used it to demonstrate that significant changes have occurred in the carbonate chemistry balance of our coastal ocean since the preindustrial era. I also developed an application of a simple machine learning technique to study our sea's biophysical dynamics. In my Master's work with Dr. Philippe Tortell, I continued the development of an automated gas sampling system for dimethylsulfide, a climate-active gas. We successfully deployed it in the Arctic. There were polar bears, which was neat. In my spare time I ride my bike medium to long distances with my partner, spend time in the mountains, read, volunteer for a local food security group, and go to the zoo to look at the shoebill storks.
